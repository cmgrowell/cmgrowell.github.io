Preparing for a Race

Summary of a session with Amber Pierce and 2025 Varsity Group

Race Support Network and Preparation * Build a supportive network to lean on. * Plan effectively.

Mindset and the Brain * Mindset shapes how we perceive challenges; our brain filters the world accordingly. * Learn the difference between reacting (impulsive) and responding (thoughtful). * The limbic system (fight or flight) activates on race day—use it to marshal resources but avoid overactivation. * Being nervous is necessary; everyone has a unique optimal performance state.

Working with Your Inner Critic * The limbic system often acts as the inner critic, detecting stress and fear. * Name the inner critic (e.g., “Regina”) to externalize and work with it. * Recognize and interrupt negative thoughts; rewire the brain by replacing or questioning them. * Shift from fight-or-flight to a social state by acknowledging the critic’s intent and refocusing.

Techniques for Optimal Performance * Breathwork: Use belly breathing to calm excitement or fear. * Mantras: Focus on a phrase to stay centered and positive. * Music: Choose tunes that help you feel energized, calm, or happy. * Practice awareness of your thoughts daily to improve performance under pressure.

Pre-Race Routine * Create a consistent pre-race routine to reduce mental load and conserve energy:

  • Prepare gear the night before.
  • Use checklists for packing and preparation.
  • Set alarms for race-day tasks.
  • A clear routine funnels energy into the race, not logistics.

Growth Mindset vs. Fixed Mindset * Embrace challenges as opportunities to grow and improve skills. * Reframe failures as learning opportunities. * Avoid comparing yourself negatively to others; see competitors as allies who push you to excel. * A growth mindset fosters curiosity and resilience, even when progress isn’t linear.

Pain and Perseverance * All athletes experience pain; how you interpret it determines its power over you. * View pain as a test of your capacity and an opportunity to grow.

Competitors and Collaboration * Competitors are allies in the pursuit of excellence. * Striving together elevates everyone’s potential.

Sleep and Long-Term Preparation * Focus on consistent sleep in the two weeks leading to the race rather than just the night before. * Calming the inner critic supports better rest.

Continuous Improvement * Always strive to learn what works and adapt. * The process of improvement is as valuable as the results.
